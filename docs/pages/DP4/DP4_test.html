🧪 BIOL341/982 – GenAI Reflection & Scientific Writing Lab

🧭 Mode: In pairs, bring your scientific report and be ready to collaborate.

This session will guide you through using GenAI to improve your scientific writing and reflect on how AI compares to human feedback.

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this lab, you will:

✅ Use 3 types of GenAI prompts to revise a scientific report
✅ Compare and reflect on AI vs. human feedback
✅ Practice ethical and responsible use of GenAI tools
✅ Submit a revised report with tracked changes

🚀 Getting Started

How to Complete This Lab

As you work through this page, open a new Microsoft Word document to record your answers. Use the checkboxes here to tick off each step as you complete it. This will help keep you on track and ensure you’ve answered everything before submission.

Before you begin:

  • ✅ Have your draft scientific report ready (ideally with comments from a peer)
  • ✅ Access a GenAI tool like Copilot (or other approved platform)
  • ✅ Use Microsoft Word or Google Docs with tracked changes enabled
Launch Copilot

Use the link below to open Microsoft Copilot in a new tab. You’ll need to be logged in with your UOW or institutional account.

🔹 Step 1: Human Feedback Summary

Task Instructions

With your partner, summarise the feedback you received from a peer or tutor.
- Be specific and use your own words
- Add how you plan to respond to this feedback

List 1–3 points of feedback you received on your report. Be specific.

💬 Great — being specific helps with revision targeting!

Describe one way you’ll apply this feedback to your report.

💬 Nice plan! Make sure to track the change clearly in your document.

🔹 Step 2: Revising with GenAI

Prompt Engineering Guide

You will now use three types of prompts to revise your report using Microsoft Copilot:

  1. 🧠 Context prompt (who the AI is)
  2. 🔧 “Do for me”
  3. 🔍 “Evaluate”
  4. 🎓 “Teach me”

Work through each one, copy the AI output, and explain how it helped.

Write a prompt that tells Copilot who it is (e.g., “You are an expert editor of a scientific journal…”)

💬 Well done! Giving AI context helps it produce better responses.

Share a prompt you used to get Copilot to rewrite or revise a section for you.

💬 Nice! Save the output and compare it to your original.

How did you use the Copilot output to change your report?

💬 Awesome — this shows thoughtful revision.

Ask Copilot to review a section. What prompt did you use?

💬 Great — feedback prompts help improve clarity.

What change did you make based on Copilot’s evaluation?

💬 Excellent — critical review improves structure.

Share a “teach me” prompt you used with Copilot (e.g., “How can I improve clarity in my methods section?”)

💬 Nice! Teaching prompts help build your writing skills.

What did you learn from Copilot’s response?

💬 Good — understanding the ‘why’ builds long-term skills.

🔹 Step 3: Reflection

📝 Reflection Questions

Answer each question below in 100–150 words. These reflections are worth 8 marks.

How did Copilot feedback compare with the feedback from your peer?

💬 Nice analysis — think about tone, specificity, and usefulness.

Which type of Copilot prompt helped most? Why?

💬 Well reasoned — it’s great to find what works for you.

Did using Copilot change how you approach writing or editing?

💬 Good insight — self-awareness is key to skill growth.

What are the benefits and risks of using GenAI like Copilot in science writing?

💬 Well balanced — keep ethics in mind.
