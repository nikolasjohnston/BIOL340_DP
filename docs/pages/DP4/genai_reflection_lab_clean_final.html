GenAI Reflection & Scientific Writing Lab

This dry practical session introduces a structured approach to using generative AI tools, such as Microsoft Copilot, to improve scientific writing. Students will critically reflect on AI-generated suggestions, compare them to human feedback, and revise their report accordingly.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will:

  • Apply structured prompts to revise scientific writing using a GenAI tool
  • Reflect on the differences between human and AI feedback
  • Practise ethical use of generative AI tools
  • Prepare and submit a revised scientific report

Getting Started

Before you begin:

  • Ensure you have a draft version of your scientific report (ideally with peer feedback)
  • Open Microsoft Copilot (or another approved AI tool)
  • Use Microsoft Word or Google Docs with tracked changes enabled
How to Complete This Lab

As you work through each section, open a Word document to record your responses. Use the text prompts as a guide. You may wish to print or save this file for your records.

Step 1: Summarise Peer Feedback

Instructions

With your partner, discuss the feedback you received on your scientific report. Summarise this feedback in your own words and outline how you plan to address it.

What feedback did you receive?

Record 1–3 key points of feedback.

How will you respond to the feedback?

Explain how you will address this feedback in your revised report.

Step 2: Use GenAI to Improve Your Writing

Instructions

You will now interact with Microsoft Copilot using four types of prompts. For each one, record what you asked the AI, what output you received, and how you applied it to your report.

2.1 Context Prompt

Write a prompt that clearly defines Copilot’s role (e.g., “You are an expert editor of a scientific journal…”).

2.2 “Do for Me” Prompt

Give Copilot a section of your writing and ask it to rewrite or improve it. Record the prompt and explain how you used the AI’s response.

2.3 “Evaluate” Prompt

Ask Copilot to evaluate a paragraph or section for clarity, tone, or scientific accuracy. Record the prompt and describe how you used the feedback.

2.4 “Teach Me” Prompt

Ask Copilot to explain how you could improve a section of your report. Write the prompt and summarise what you learned.

Step 3: Reflection

Answer the following questions in 100–150 words each.

Compare Feedback

How did the AI-generated feedback compare to the feedback provided by your peer?

Most Useful Prompt

Which type of prompt did you find most useful for improving your writing? Why?

Impact on Writing

Did using Copilot influence how you approach writing or editing in science?

Benefits and Limitations

What are the strengths and weaknesses of using GenAI tools in scientific writing?

Submission Checklist

Before submitting your work:

  • Ensure tracked changes are turned on in your revised scientific report
  • Complete your response worksheet with peer feedback and GenAI prompts
  • Include any relevant screenshots or transcripts of Copilot interactions in an appendix

Submit your materials via Moodle by the due date.

Optional Extension

Reflect with your partner on ethical concerns:

What would be considered an inappropriate use of Copilot in the context of scientific writing? List 2–3 examples and include them in your appendix.
